## 💬 What would you like to talk about?

I hope this space becomes more than just a 'repository of self-records'—a connection point with people looking in the same direction. I especially welcome topics like the following.

### 🤝 Things we can discuss together

- **Empathy or counterarguments regarding investment philosophy** — Serious questions like, "I think this way, how does it differ from your KCA strategy?" are most welcome. Healthy disagreements teach us more than agreement.
- **Honest confessions in the face of market fear** — The experience of hands shaking on days when stock prices plummeted, and the reasons we held on anyway. I believe these stories accumulate to become strong anchors for one another.
- **Book recommendations and sharing reading notes** — Let's talk about books related to investing, asset allocation, economics, and attitudes toward life. A good book can sometimes prevent a bad investment decision.
- **Reporting site errors and feedback** — Please let me know if you find incorrect information or broken pages. It's a great help in making a better site.
- **Collaboration and interview proposals** — Feel free to contact me if you'd like to develop the content of this site together or introduce it on other channels.

## 🙋 About the creator of this site

I am an 18-year corporate employee and a father of two daughters. I started long-term investing in earnest in 2021, and beyond simply preparing for retirement, I am practicing asset planning with the grand dream of a **'One Billion Dollar Donation'**.

This site is a record of that journey. I started it not to show anyone, but as an anchor to hold myself steady when the market shakes. However, I thought it would be wonderful if it could serve as a small compass for those grappling with the same concerns.

Investing can be a lonely battle. Especially on days when the market crashes and everyone around you is selling stocks, holding on alone is much harder than you might think. That's why I value connections with people who are quietly walking their own paths while looking in the same direction.

**Stay the Course.** 🌿
