# Chapter 7. The Difference Between Robots and Humans, and the Birth of Smart Beta

![Dot-com Bubble and Mentality](/evo-ch7-cover.png)

## Introduction: The Index Fund is Still the Greatest Invention

Through the journey up to Chapter 6, we confirmed why the 'Market Index (Index Fund)' is the perfect basecamp for investing. So, during massive crashes like the 2000 Dot-com Bubble and the 2008 Global Financial Crisis, did index investors all go bankrupt and disappear?

Not at all. When the accounts of regular investors chasing theme stocks and soaring trends literally became scrap paper (delisted) and were permanently expelled from the market, the Bogleheads who silently held onto the market index suffered the pain of their accounts halving, but ultimately became the final victors by breaking through all-time highs along with the market's upward trend. **John Bogle's philosophy of "Don't try to beat the market; own the entire market" remains an unchanging truth even in crashing markets.**

The problem was not the weapon called the index fund, but the **'human psychology'** holding it.

---

## 1. Two Bitter Crises: Machines Endure, but Humans Collapse

An index fund is a perfect machine with no emotions. However, we who operate this machine are frail humans vulnerable to fear and greed. The two most horrific pages in financial history showed how brutally the structural characteristics of index funds test human mentality.

* **2000 Dot-com Bubble (The Test of Greed):** Index funds do not consider a company's true value (fundamentals); they only include the market based on market capitalization (stock price) ratios. During the Dot-com Bubble, when the madness of the public caused the stock prices of deficit-ridden internet companies to skyrocket by dozens of times, the index fund automatically loaded up heavily on these bubbly garbage stocks at their peak. When the bubble burst, the index collapsed mercilessly.
* **2008 Global Financial Crisis (The Test of Fear):** Conversely, during the financial crisis, the insides of giant traditional financial institutions that had grown their size over a long time were rotting with toxic subprime loans. However, the index fund kept these insolvent companies in the index until they were completely delisted, plummeting together like a waterfall.

If you were a robot, you could look at an account showing -50% and silently wait for 10 years. But when your life savings are halved and the news screams every day that capitalism is doomed, almost no regular investor could maintain their reason and endure. Countless Bogleheads eventually succumbed to fear, throwing away their index funds at the bottom and becoming losers themselves.

---

## 2. Smart Beta: Not Discarding the Index, but 'Complementing' It

In this horrific history, investors learned a crucial lesson.
*"The index fund (market index) remains the eternal Core of investing. But for us humans to endure the agony of a massive crash and stay in the market until the end, we need a **Shock Absorber** that filters out market madness (bubbles) and insolvency to cushion the blow to our mentality!"*

The answer that emerged to this was **'Smart Beta'**.
In 2005, Rob Arnott of Research Affiliates and others created 'Fundamental Indexing', which determines the weight of companies based on real numbers recorded in accounting books—namely **fundamentals like revenue, book value, dividends, and earnings**—instead of 'stock prices (market cap)' tainted with human greed.

Smart Beta is not an enemy trying to replace the index fund.
1. When stock prices become more expensive than their true value (like the Dot-com Bubble), it reduces the weight of those stocks, and
2. It completely filters out empty companies that cannot make money (like the Financial Crisis),
acting as a **sturdy sub-weapon (auxiliary weapon)** that mitigates the extreme volatility index funds experience during crises.

## Conclusion and Next Chapter Preview: Raising a Shield to Survive to the End

My investment philosophy remains unshaken. The solid ground that forms the foundation of everything is still the **'Market Index (Market Beta)'**.
However, because I am a frail human, not a robot, I needed a 'safety net' to prevent my mentality from collapsing and making me leave the market when hells like the Dot-com Bubble and Financial Crisis return.

A Smart Beta that filters not just companies with large market caps, but **companies with sturdy Quality that won't shake in a crisis and Value that will constantly plug cash flow (dividends) into my account**.

Integrating this human psychological defense mechanism with the technology of Smart Beta, **finally, in 2011, the most perfect defensive weapon in modern investment history was born**.
