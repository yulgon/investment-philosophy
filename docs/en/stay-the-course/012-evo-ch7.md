# Chapter 7. The Tragedy of the Dot-com Bubble and the Birth of Smart Beta

![Tragedy of the Dot-com Bubble](/evo-ch7-cover.png)

## Introduction: The Fatal Weakness of the Invincible Basecamp (Index Fund)
Numerous Bogleheads around the world, including myself, have blindly trusted 'Market-Cap Weighting'. If a great company's stock price rises and its market cap grows, the index fund automatically increases its weight, and if a declining company's stock falls, it reduces its weight. What a perfect 'automatic purification system'!
We firmly believed, "I don't need to pick stocks. I just need to sleep inside this magnificent basecamp."

However, when I reviewed the most horrific page in financial history, the 2000 **'Dot-com Bubble'**, my blind faith was miserably shattered. The chilling dark side of the seemingly perfect automatic purification system was exposed.

---

## 1. The Curse of the Machine that Follows Price

During the Dot-com Bubble, the public lost its mind and was blinded by greed. Even for garbage companies drowning in deficits with zero revenue, just appending '.com' to the company name caused their stock prices to skyrocket by dozens of times overnight.

Right at this point, the index fund's automatic purification system turned into a demon.
* **A Machine that Sweeps up Bubbles:** An index fund completely ignores whether a company is actually making money (fundamentals). It ignorantly increases the weight of a company just because its 'stock price (market cap)' is high.
* **Completion of the Tragedy:** As large internet tech stocks became insanely bubbly, their weight in the S&P 500 index abnormally expanded. On the other hand, small and solid value stocks (Small-Cap Value), completely neglected by market interest, were rolling around at bargain prices. However, market index investors focused on large caps were sleeping soundly thinking, "I bought the market, so I'm safe," while in reality, **they were buying the most bubbled, large garbage stocks in the market with their own money.**

Eventually, in March 2000, when the bubble burst, the S&P 500 was halved. The place we believed was a basecamp was actually a powder keg filled with bubbles.

---

## 2. Discard Price and Grasp Fundamentals

Analyzing the charts of the Dot-com Bubble, I recalled the lessons of behavioral finance (Chapter 3) once again.
*"Price is easily manipulated and distorted by human greed and fear. If I determine the weight of companies based on bubbled prices, I will eventually get bitten at the peak of the bubble!"*

In 2005, after experiencing this historical tragedy, **Rob Arnott**, founder of Research Affiliates, threw a revolutionary solution to investors agonizing like me.

*"Let's completely ignore 'price', which is tainted with human madness. Instead, let's determine the weight of companies based on real numbers recorded in accounting books, namely **fundamentals like revenue, book value, and dividends**!"*

This was the genesis of what is called **'Fundamental Indexing'**.

---

## 3. Smart Beta: An Automatic Defense System to Protect Your Mentality

The emergence of fundamental indexing was a ray of light for passive investors.
This method possessed an amazing automatic defense capability. If a stock price becomes more expensive than the company's true value (fundamentals/bubble), it mechanically reduces the weight of that stock, and if the stock price becomes cheaper than its fundamentals (undervaluation/oversold), it buys more of it.

In other words, a **perfect bot that mechanically executes 'Value Investing'**, which Eugene Fama proved in Chapter 4, without the intervention of my emotions was born.

Innovative funds that follow the market average (Beta) while adding the factor of fundamentals (Alpha) to smartly beat the market began to be called **'Smart Beta'**. Finally, the outline of a system that would protect me from the bubble of price (Price) steeped in greed was formed.

---

## Conclusion and Next Chapter Preview: The Ultimate Shield Forged by the Most Horrific Crash

If the history of the Dot-com Bubble instilled in me the philosophy of 'fundamentals', another severe hammering was needed for this philosophy to be forged into a practically useful 'ultimate shield'.

In 2008, right before I earnestly jumped into the stock market, the **'Global Financial Crisis (Subprime Mortgage Crisis)'** erupted, almost collapsing the entire global capitalist system. The S&P 500 recorded a hellish crash of -56%.

This blood-red history gave me a bitter lesson. Simple fundamentals alone are not enough. We desperately needed a **sturdy defense shield (Quality & Value) that would not collapse in any crisis and could plug blood-like cash into my account**.

In this way, the tears of blood from 2008, the value factor, and the technology of smart beta were all integrated, and **finally, in 2011, the most perfect defensive weapon in modern investment history was born**.
