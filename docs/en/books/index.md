# 📚 My Investment Reading Notes

> "Successful investing begins by standing on the shoulders of giants."

This is a list of masterpieces that have deeply inspired me since 2021, shaping my investment philosophy and asset allocation strategies.  
I have compiled the books I take out to read and use as a compass whenever shaken by market volatility.

> [!NOTE] How this page is edited
> This is not a collection of publisher blurbs. The **Personal Note** on each card records what the book changed in my own investment rules and, where relevant, what I read with caution or disagreement. The quiz is a simple navigation tool connecting answers to tags; it does not rank books objectively.

## Three reading paths by purpose

- **If you are building principles for the first time:** read index and allocation, then costs, then behavioral bias.
- **If drawdowns are the hardest part:** begin with psychology and decision-making before product-focused books.
- **If you want cash flow:** compare books that discuss taxes, concentration risk, and total return—not only the appeal of dividends.
